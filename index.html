2025, Daniel Zhu, Ravi Patel

Abstract: This paper details the technical implementation of autonomous driving systems across two Formula SAE (FSAE) vehicles, developed by Carnegie Mellon Racing (CMR). As the first team to successfully demonstrate a driverless FSAE vehicle at an SAE-sanctioned event in the United States, CMR has established itself as a leader among university teams in the integration of advanced perception, path planning, and control systems, advancing the field of autonomous vehicles within the unique challenges of the Formula SAE competition's technical and regulatory frameworks. We present an in-depth discussion of the system architecture, including the sensor suite, embedded computing, and actuation mechanisms required for real-time autonomous operation. Our software stack is explored in detail, covering the perception pipeline for environment understanding, localization and mapping techniques, motion planning strategies, and control algorithms. Additionally, we discuss challenges encountered during system integration, hardware-software interfacing, and debugging. Lessons learned from deployment, testing strategies, and potential improvements for future iterations are also addressed. By documenting the engineering approaches and solutions developed throughout this process, this paper provides valuable insights for student researchers and engineers working on autonomous vehicle systems, particularly in the context of FSAE driverless competitions. The work presented here establishes a foundation for future teams seeking to push the boundaries of autonomy in student-built race cars.
